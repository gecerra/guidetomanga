Resources

Check out these resources to expand your collections and programming ideas!

Related Books

Gastronogeek Anime Cookbook - Looking to incorporate some cooking programs? This book features a variety of recipes from different anime and manga, so you can make all of your favorite dishes. The author, Thibaud Villanova, has a variety of other geek-related cookbooks available, including the recently released The Unofficial Ghibli Cookbook. If you have a lot of anime or video game fans in your library, these books make solid additions to your collection!

Kawaii Doggies - Need some drawing inspiration? With 75 different dogs to try and draw, you could use this book as inspiration for any number of creative projects. My personal favorite is drawing them on cards and then trading with others.

Manga in Theory and Practice - A book of wise manga-making words from the author of Jojo's Bizarre Adventure. Fans will learn all about Araki's storytelling techniques, the process behind his character creation, and other aspects behind making manga. I found this to be a very fascinating read, and would highly recommend to fans of Jojo's, those who would like to create their own manga, or for anyone that enjoys reading about the creative process.

Publishers

Kodansha: They carry some big name titles like Sailor Moon, Attack on Titan, and Blue Lock. They also offer a variety of non-manga titles related to Japanese culture, such as light novels, language-learning, and crafts.

VIZ Media: Home to big Shonen Jump titles, including Demon Slayer, One Piece, and My Hero Academia. If you're looking to familiarize yourself with some of the more popular titles, this is a good place to start.

Seven Seas Entertainment: If you're a fan of cats, you'll have plenty of options to pick from here. They also have a pretty big listing of titles for teens, as well as light novels. One of my personal favorite manga titles from them is Magical Angel Creamy Mami and the Spoiled Princess.

Yen Press: With six different imprints, they cover manga, light-novels, and audiobooks. Their imprint Ize Press offers manhwa, or Korean comics, which have started to gain some popularity in the west.

TokyoPop: Has a total of 3 imprints, including TokyoPop, LoveLove, and Disney Manga. I would suggest looking at the latter if you're looking to add more manga for younger readers.
